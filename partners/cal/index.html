The script (read once before you start, don't memorize)
📜 The 6-stage cold call script

Stage 1: Opening (5-10 sec)

Identify yourself. Name their shop by name. Ask permission.

"Hey [First Name], this is Cal. I'm calling about [Shop Name], got thirty seconds?"

If they say "what's this about", that's a yes wearing a costume. Continue.

If they say "no": "Quickest pitch you've ever heard, twenty seconds. Then I'm out." Most will give it.

Stage 2: Wedge (20-30 sec)

Don't pitch the product yet. Get them to feel the pain.

"Quick question. When somebody lands on [Shop Name]'s website at ten o'clock at night with a busted water heater, what happens?"

Pause. Let them answer. Don't fill the silence.

Stage 3: Pitch (30 sec)

Now name the product. Keep it short.

"I work with Cole at StillOpen. He built an AI front desk for shops like yours. It lives on your website as a chat. When someone lands at 10pm, it answers them in your shop's voice with your prices, your hours, your service area. Captures the lead, books the job into your calendar, texts you. Two-ninety-seven to install, forty-seven a month after a 30-day trial."

Stage 4: Demo Ask (the kill move)

"Want me to set one up on YOUR site right now? Takes thirty seconds, you'll see exactly what your customers would see."

Stage 5: Demo walk-through

Walk them to stillopen.ai. Tell them to paste their site URL in the demo box. Wait silently. When the chat appears, tell them to ask "how much for a drain cleaning?" like a customer would.

After they see their own bot answer with their own prices: shut up. Let them see it.

Stage 6: Close

"That's what's on your site by Monday if we kick this off today. Two-ninety-seven to install, forty-seven a month with a 30-day trial. Want to grab a payment link or you got questions first?"

If they say "Send me the link": text Cole right then, he sends the Stripe link to the plumber. You text the plumber: "Cole's sending the link in a sec, look for cole@stillopen.ai."

What NOT to say (ever)

  • "Quick question" twice in the same call
  • "How are you doing today?"
  • "It's a game-changer"
  • "Powered by AI" (it's just a chat that knows their prices)
  • "Just imagine..."
  • Apologies for taking their time (you're solving their problem)
🥊 Top 8 objection handlers

Don't memorize the words. Memorize the shape: agree, reframe, demo, ask.

1. "It's too expensive."

"Yeah, $297 up front is real money. Quick math, what's your average ticket on a drain clean? [Wait.] OK so one job covers the install and the first six months of monthly. The $47/mo has a 30-day trial. Your only at-risk dollar is the install. If it doesn't book you a single job in the first month, you're out $297 and you keep the bot another 30 days."

2. "I have a guy already."

"Smart, those guys are valuable. What do they handle for you, Google ads, SEO? [Wait.] OK so they drive people TO your site. I'm the part where the visitor LANDS at 9pm and gets a real conversation instead of a contact form. We're not the same thing. Want to see what your visitors are seeing right now?"

3. "Sounds like every other AI scam call."

"Fair, you probably get five of these a week. Forget the pitch. Open a browser, go to stillopen dot a-i, paste your URL in the demo box. If you don't see something useful in 30 seconds, hang up and we're done. I'll wait while you type."

4. "I don't do the computer stuff. My wife does the website."

"Totally fair. This is the kind of thing your wife handles. Two options. I can three-way her on this call right now, or I email her the demo link with a five-line summary. Which one's easier?"

5. "We don't do the AI thing."

"Yeah, the AI thing's gotten loud. Real talk, call it a chat that knows your prices. That's what it is. It's not making appointments on its own and it's not pretending to be you on calls. It just answers questions on your website with the info you've already put on your site. Want to see one?"

6. "Just send me some info."

"I can. Couple things, the demo takes 30 seconds and shows you more than my email could. Got a browser open?" If still no: "OK, what's your email? I'll send the demo link plus a one-paragraph summary."

7. "How is this different from Workiz / Housecall Pro?"

"Good question. Those are full operating systems, scheduling, dispatching, invoicing. Real software, mostly $200-400/mo, often a contract. We don't replace those. We're just the front-desk piece. $47/mo, no contract, on top of whatever else you use. If you've got Workiz, this is the missing chat layer. If you don't, this is the cheapest way to start catching after-hours leads."

8. "I need to think about it."

"Totally fair. One favor, what's the one thing you'd need answered to feel good about it? Because if it's price or the AI part or trust, I can probably answer it right now and save you a callback."

If still vague: "OK, when's good for me to follow up, Monday or Wednesday?" Schedule the callback. Stop pushing.

The meta-rule

When in doubt: stop talking and ask a question. Plumbers fill silence with information. Information beats your monologue every time.
