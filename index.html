What it does

Three things, done right.

1

It answers the phone when you can't.

The chat widget sits on your existing website. A customer comes in at 7 PM or 2 AM or Sunday afternoon. The bot answers their questions using your real prices and your real service menu. No made-up numbers. No generic FAQ. No "our agents are offline" dead end.

2

It books the job into your calendar.

When the customer wants to book, the bot pulls your real availability from Google Calendar and drops the appointment in. Customer name, phone, address, problem description, time window. You wake up to a real event on your calendar, not an email you have to chase.

3

It takes a deposit on your Stripe.

For emergency work, the bot asks for a small deposit before it confirms. Not to nickel-and-dime the customer. To hold the slot and commit a real human to the job. The customer pays through your Stripe account. Money hits your bank. The customer shows up. Roto-Rooter doesn't get the call.
