What it does

Three things, done right.

1

It catches the website visitor your phone can't.

The chat widget sits on your existing website. A customer comes in at 7 PM or 2 AM or Sunday afternoon. The bot answers their questions using your real prices and your real service menu. No made-up numbers. No generic FAQ. No "our agents are offline" dead end.

2

It books the job into your calendar.

When the customer wants to book, the bot pulls your real availability from Google Calendar and drops the appointment in. Customer name, phone, address, problem description, time window. You wake up to a real event on your calendar, not an email you have to chase.

3

It takes a deposit on your Stripe.

For emergency work, the bot asks for a small deposit before it confirms. Not to nickel-and-dime the customer. To hold the slot and commit a real human to the job. The customer pays through your Stripe account. Money hits your bank. The customer shows up. Roto-Rooter doesn't get the call.

Who it's for

Built for the shop owner who answers their own phone.

You run 1-5 trucks. You answer the phone when it rings because you're the business. You have a website. You've got a Google Calendar or a paper calendar you're ready to move off of. You've got a Stripe account or you're willing to set one up in about 4 minutes. That's it. If you can use email, you can use StillOpen.

StillOpen isn't a dispatch system. It isn't a CRM. It isn't an answering service that charges you $400 a month and still can't book or collect. It doesn't pick up your phone, and it isn't a chatbot widget that just forwards leads to an email inbox you check once a day. It's a chat on your website that catches the visitor after your phone goes to voicemail, books the job, and takes a Stripe deposit before they call your competitor. That's what it does. That's all it does.

How it works

Setup takes 15 minutes. Not 15 days.

Step 01

Sign up and connect your tools.

Email, Google Calendar, Stripe. OAuth does the handshakes. You don't have to hire anybody to set it up, and you don't have to hire me.

Step 02

Point it at your website.

The bot reads your site and pulls your prices, your service menu, your service area, your hours. You review what it learned and fix anything it got wrong.

Step 03

Drop the snippet on your site.

One line of code you copy and paste. If that sentence sounds scary, your web guy can do it in 30 seconds. The bot's live the moment the snippet lands.
